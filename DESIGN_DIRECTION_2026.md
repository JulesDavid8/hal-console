# H.A.L. Compass — Design Direction (Quiet Authority)

**Approved Direction**: "Quiet Authority"

## Core Positioning
H.A.L. Compass should feel like the **calm, precise, and quietly powerful** workstation that serious investors use when they want to understand what the smartest money is actually doing.

It must feel:
- **Familiar** to professional users of Bloomberg, Thinkorswim, and TradingView.
- **Elevated and modern** — more refined, clearer, and more thoughtful than those tools.
- **Trustworthy above all else**.

## Visual Language

### Color Strategy (Most Important Change)

**Rule**: Color should almost always carry meaning. The previous heavy green approach broke this rule.

**Base Layer**
- Deep cool charcoal with subtle blue undertones.
- Very low saturation. Almost no green in the backgrounds.

**Primary Accent**
- A sophisticated, slightly cool teal (#00B8A6 range).
- This is our main brand color. It nods to classic terminals without feeling retro or cheap.

**Semantic Colors (Used deliberately and sparingly)**
- Green (#22C55E): Only for clear bullish / accumulation signals.
- Red (#F87171): Only for clear bearish / distribution signals.
- Amber (#FBBF24): Warnings, attention, high-priority items.
- Teal/Cyan: Data highlights, technical elements, secondary actions.

**Text**
- High contrast neutral white for primary text.
- Much stronger, more professional muted gray for secondary text.

### Typography

- **UI & Reading**: Inter (or similar high-quality sans) — excellent legibility at small sizes and high density.
- **Data, Numbers, Commands, Tickers**: Strong monospace (Share Tech Mono or similar).
- **Headers**: Inter with slightly increased weight and tracking for authority.

### Density & Space

- Information density should be high (this is a pro tool).
- However, we will use excellent visual hierarchy, clear sectioning, and generous internal padding so it never feels cramped or noisy.
- Cards and sections should have clear separation and breathing room.

### Tone & Feeling

- Quiet confidence.
- Professional without being cold.
- Modern without being trendy.
- Powerful without being intimidating.
- "This is the tool the serious people use."

4. **Design System** (must reflect the new quality bar)

---

## Success Criteria (Live Tester Perspective)

When a professional trader opens the app, they should feel:

- "This feels serious and high-quality."
- "I can find what I need quickly."
- "The data actually feels trustworthy and well-presented."
- "This is better than what I’ve used before."

Not:
- "This looks like another green terminal toy."
- "Everything is green and hard to read."
- "It feels cheap or inconsistent."

---

This direction will be the north star for all future UI work.
